Aditya Jaishankar

Biography

I am currently a Data Science Fellow at Insight Data Science. I am working on applying NLP techniques such as Latent Dirichlet Allocation, word2vec embeddings, and binary classification using support vector machines on millions of scraped tweets to identify topics of interest to occasional farmers markets shoppers. Providing this input to farmers market managers and organizers can help them make real-time, local, data-driven decisions on relevant community events to host at the market. This adds an additional draw for shoppers, and increasing market attendance. A demo of the product is available (here). A presentation is available here. The Github repo is available (here).

I am a soft matter physicist and surface scientist with experience in theoretical, computational (numerical) and experimental work. At ExxonMobil Corporate Strategic Research Lab I work on mathematical models to enable scalable and economically viable solutions for the capture of CO2 directly from air. I also work on developing molecularly thick self-assembled surfaces that provide ultra-low friction for more fuel efficient engines.

I received a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in June 2014. I worked with Professor Gareth McKinley in the Non-Newtonian Fluid Dynamics Group. I was a recipient of MIT Presidential Fellowship.

My thesis on The Linear and Nonlinear Rheology of Multiscale Complex Fluids developed theoretical models for describing the behavior of soft materials and complex fluids whose building blocks are arranged in fractal patterns. You can read more about my research here.

I also studied the effect pre-shearing a polymer melt filled with particles has on subsequent extensional stretching deformation. To prevent gravity-induced sagging of the stretched polymer filament, the experiments had to be performed in a microgravity environment. These experiments were performed by astronaut Mike Fossum aboard the International Space Station's Microgravity Science Glovebox. You can read a summary of the Shear History Extensional Rheology Experiment.

Earlier, I graduated from the Department of Mechanical Engineering at IIT Madras with a Bachelor of Technology degree.
